My Football Journey

From muddy fields to third-division matches — and everything in between.

Bjarg

Where it all started. I was just a kid learning how to kick a ball without falling over.

Brann

Joining Brann’s youth academy was my first exposure to elite football. The bar was high—and I loved it.

Fana

At Fana, I focused on development, learned patience, and pushed through injury setbacks.

Fyllingsdalen

A club with ambition. This is where I started to think like a team player—not just an individual.

Lyngbø

Here, I became captain. I learned to lead from the front, both in wins and in tough seasons.

My football story began like many others — with a ball, a dream, and two shoes way too big for me. I was five years old when I joined my first team. Back then, I had no idea that football would shape my mindset, my friendships, and my leadership skills for the years to come.

From the youth system at SK Brann to taking the captain’s armband at Lyngbø IL, every chapter had its own lessons. Brann gave me a taste of elite youth football, while Lyngbø taught me how to lead a team through both wins and losses.

“Football taught me to fight for others, not just for myself.”

I still carry that team-first mentality with me, whether I'm working on a group project or helping lead a student organization. Football was never just about goals — it was about grit, community, and growth.

These days, I don’t train five times a week anymore. But I do bring the same discipline and energy into everything I do — from auditing oil companies at EY to chasing impact in student-led events.
